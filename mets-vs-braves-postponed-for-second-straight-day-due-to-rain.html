Mets vs. Braves postponed for second straight day due to rain

Explore More

The steady stream of rain in New York has washed out the Mets-Braves game once again.

The Mets announced their Sunday afternoon game against the Braves has been postponed and will be made up as part of a single-admission doubleheader on Monday at 1:10 p.m.

Saturday’s game was also postponed due to rain, and that game will be made up as part of a split-admission doubleheader on Aug. 12.

Tickets to Sunday’s originally scheduled game aren’t valid for admission to Monday’s doubleheader.

Fans will receive a digital voucher that can be exchanged for a ticket to a future game.

Saturday’s rainout pushed back the return of Max Scherzer, who’s 10-game suspension for violating MLB’s sticky-stuff rules won’t be complete until the end of the Braves series, if both games are played Monday when the forecast is clearer.

Scherzer is scheduled to return on Tuesday in Detroit.
